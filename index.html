Hello!

I'm a fourth-year Computer Science student at Middlebury College, Class of 2020.5. My main interests are in computer vision, image processing, and remote sensing.

For my senior thesis, I'm studying despeckling methods for multitemporal Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) image datasets. I'm working on a diffusion-based despeckling algorithm that leverages temporal information (as well as spatial information) to improve the quality of the denoised result, especially for small-scale features.

Recent Activity

  • I spent Summer 2020 working on remote sensing research with Professor Andrea Vaccari. Our goal was to classify pavement quality based on X-band Synthetic Aperture Radar amplitude data. We compared pavement data collected by vehicle with SAR data from the COSMO-SkyMed satellite. [group github]
  • For a recent data science project, I explored reports of very large flocks of birds in the eBird basic dataset with the goal of understanding trends in the way birders count, estimate, and report very large numbers. [shiny app] [code] [dataset]
  • For my senior seminar capstone project, I compared several machine learning methods for automatically classifying land cover (specifically, wetland areas) based on LiDAR data and optical imagery. [abstract] [mid-project poster]
  • At the end of my semester abroad in Spring of 2019, I worked as a research assistant at Laboratoire d'Informatique et d'Automatique pour des Systèmes at l'Université de Poitiers, where I constructed a prototype network of IoT devices using the LoRaWAN protocol. [abstract (en/fr)]
